# The Arc

If you've picked this book up it probably means that either you've been asked to deliver the impossible or you've asked somebody else to deliver the impossible. You wouldn't feel challenged if what was being asked for were possible. Possible things are boring. The price of delivering possible things is understood. But also the value of possible things is understood. When you ask for a coffee in your favourite barista bar, no matter how complicated that coffee, you're very likely to get the coffee. You're not likely, you're really not likely, to get a cup full of gold or diamonds. There is little risk involved.

By contrast, on the most ordinary, everyday software development project, the costs are often not understood, the risks have neither been explored nor acknowledged and the value is vague. Even the smallest software development projects can be a monumental adventure.
