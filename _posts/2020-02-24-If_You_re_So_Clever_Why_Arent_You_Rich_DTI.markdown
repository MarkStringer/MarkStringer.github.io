This is a very good question. And I'm not sure that I have an answer to it.

But this is a good question to ask anyone who has taken it upon themselves to tell other people what will and won't work in any walk of life. 

No, that's not true. I think I *do* have a couple of answers. 

Answer number one is related to something that we've discussed here: the sociopathic distance. I *think* to be rich you need to be able to take risks in such a way that you are handsomely rewarded when things go well and not punished unduly (financially, emotionally, legally) when things go badly. The whole system of corporations and limited companies is essentially set up for this. 

I think that some of the people who we see as being rich because they are super clever are, no doubt, clever guys, but also they are the ones who did well. Their projects did well.

And in this there might be an important point about project management, but also a point about why I'm not rich.

People who have made money have either got lucky and had one particular project pay off beyond anyone's wildest dreams, or they understand that embarking on ventures is as much about failure as it is about success. Embarking on projects is a risky business. When a project fails, they distance themselves from it, when a project succeeds, they make sure that they take the rewards and the credit.
