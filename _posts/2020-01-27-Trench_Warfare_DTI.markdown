# Trench Warfare

> "In football everything is complicated by the presence of the opposite team" - Jean Paul Sartre.

What you are wanting to avoid. At least what *I'm* wanting to avoid, in a project is trench warfare. My definition of trench warfare, is this:

Trench warfare is a situation where any progress or any increase in value is extracted only at an enormous cost and only very slowly. It's also in the nature of trench warfare that any gains that might be made also open up the possibility of substantial losses. 

In war, these forces that are opposed, are, of course, literally forces. But of course, above both of the dug-in, opposing forces is a big idea - the big prize of winning of being the victors. 

In a software development project, there's similar three way dynamic. We have the team that is trying to make the software happen and they are opposing a deeply powerful force - *reality*.

The real question about trench warfare in software projects is why they're so attractive to senior management. Which I suppose might be related to the question of why attrition wars are so attractive to Generals.
