# A Brief Aside - It's not the End of the World

Of course you want your project to be a success, but every now and then it might be worth just walking through why it won't be the end of the world if it's the end of this project. 

Of course, there will be certain people who want to "up the stakes" and make it sound like it *will* be the end of the world if a project isn't delivered on time. 

But if a project *is* failing, here are some things that you should *absolutely* do and in this order.

1. Look after your health
No need to go a crazy diet, no need to signup for an ultramarathon. Maybe some need to go for a stroll at lunchtime or do whatever mild exercise it is that you've done in the past (I got through the worst project that I've ever worked on with yoga).

If you see any indications that your health is suffering, get them checked out.

2. Look after your relationships 
Meet people for lunch, meet people for a drink after work, meet people for coffee. Make sure these are non-work people. 

3. Look after your network
Go to a meetup. Go to a conference. For me, there are two kinds of conferences, punters (people who you might work for) and practitioners (people who do what you do). 

4. Look after your career
Go to a meetup, pay for yourself to go on a training course if the company that you're working for won't pay for it.

5. Have the courage to do absolutely nothing
Sit around and slack. 

6. Creative playdate
The is an idea that I got from reading "The Artist's" way - but this isn't just for creative people. This is just doing *something* that's for you. 

This is what's going to happen if this project fails: you will get a job on another project.

This is also what's going to happen if this project fails: you will have more experience which you will be able to use to either 
A. Make this project more likely of success
B. Realise that this projec isn't going to succeed and get out it and move on to something more promising.
