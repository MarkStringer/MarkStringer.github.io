Today is Sunday February the 9th

So I don't know if this is already to "meta" but yesterday when I was writing about junking my own project, but I had the rogue thought that I could do the same to this project - writing this book. 

### 1) Find out how much work is involved 2) Show actual progress through that work
I don't know how much work is involved. I'll know more after I've made it to the fifty page alpha. 

#### What is your actual progress compared to reality?
* So far I've written about 12 pages (8-9 percent of what is needed - but it's not in a fit state for publishing).

### Describe any obstacles
* I'm busy with at least three or four other things (Greek, work, comedy, walking). 

* I don't have confidence/experience that I can actually deliver a book.

#### What are you doing to discover obstacles?
Well, I'm doing this, which I didn't think I would do.

#### What are you doing to address/remove obstacles?
Truth be told, nothing much at the moment.

* I do have this idea of asking people to be alpha readers - I think that might be Steve and Themis.

* Before I can show anything to alpha readers I need to get into the habit of printing some of this out and editing it on the bus

### Find out how similar projects performed
I know that similar projects that I've done have run into the sand. I have 10-50 pages of several books. I can't even tell you why they stopped at that point. They just kind of gave up the ghost. One thing that happens is that my work situation changes and so I my work rhythm is different or I have far less time and the book stops at that point. The other thing that happens is that the book gets to a length where it becomes unwieldy.

I don't know what the structure is, I fear that I've run out of things to say. The phrase that has just popped into my head is that "I get to the edge of the desert and get scared and go home."

#### What could you do differently to make sure that your project has a better chance

### Describe the value stream
People who are actually have the job of delivering projects and people who want other people to deliver projects for them read the book. As a result of this several things happen:

1) They recommend the book to other people - so I sell books
2) They still struggle so they ring me and I do consultancy for them
3) I end up running workshops and courses

#### What are you doing to improve the value stream
I've done one thing so far, which is to talk about it at Agile Stand-up. The next thing that I need to do is to recruit "Alpha Readers".

### What nagging doubt do you have about this value stream? 
E.g. with a the personal loan project that I worked on the nagging doubt was that there was no way to make it profitable.

With this book, there's the nagging doubt that I would put enormous amounts of energy into it and not only no-one would read it, but no-one get to hear of it, which is even worse.

There's also a nagging doubt that it's not the kind of non-fiction book that I'd like to *read*. I read and enjoyed Extreme Programming Explained, I read and enjoyed Scrum and XP from the trenches. I'm reading and enjoying "A Life of One's own". Would it be anything like that? I think if it is going to be like that it has to have a way of bringing people, almost hypnotically into every chapter. 

### How will you know when you're done?

I'll have a 150 page e-book that has an ISBN which is available for download as an ebook for kindle (hopefully on the amazon site) and is also available for publish on demand. 

### What will happen after you're done?

I'll be embarrassed how long it's taken and I'll be embarrassed at what's been created because I'll be able to see all of the flaws in it. That's exactly how I feel about animals, it's exactly how I feel about the training course. 

I'm hoping that that will result in consultancy gigs. I suppose what I really need is a course that I can teach other people to do that results in me getting a fee. 

### How do you feel right now?

Depressed. But I know that this is the depression that comes from looking at how things actually are.
